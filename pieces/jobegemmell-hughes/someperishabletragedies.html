Jobe Gemmell-Hughes

Some Perishable Tragedies


The Palacio de Cristal’s translucent panes, soft inner part of this Spanish afternoon
spattered the Mudéjar tiled entrance with wet feathered birds
interrupted by pitiful deaths
                                                 amid virginal flights.

A blind man playing odes on crystal jars feels up
                                                 the funny sufferings.

Nearby, the black swan swims circling the acidic green sludge like oxidized metal growing ever
more tired of the curvilinear modernist forms
                                                 she makes in her wake

her in and of itself-ness obscured by moralized essences prescribed

she appeared the saddest of symbols
                                                 to languor in our muck.
